Why the browser

Built for the trace, not the tabs.

A real browser, re-engineered around one job: putting a whole person in front of you in minutes.

One address bar, every source

Type any identifier — name, number, email, handle or photo. The browser fans the query out across social, telco, records and the open web simultaneously and ranks the matches by confidence.

Anonymous by default

Every session runs behind a clean, disposable identity. No cookies, no fingerprint, no trail back to you or your subject. Investigate without tipping anyone off.

Evidence-ready capture

Timestamp, screenshot and log every result to a court-ready dossier as you go. Export a clean PDF trace with sources cited — no copy-paste, no reconstruction later.

Live cross-referencing

As matches land, the browser links them into one profile — same face, same number, same person across platforms — so you see the connections, not a pile of tabs.

Onshore & compliant

All processing and storage stays in New Zealand, under the Privacy Act 2020 and PSPLA licensing. Every lookup is logged for your compliance record.

Minutes, not weeks

No dead ends, no cyber jargon. Average time from a single detail to a confirmed match is under two minutes. Built for the field, not the lab.

How it works

Three steps to a full picture

01

Drop in one detail

Open a new trace and type whatever you have — a phone number scribbled on a job sheet, an email, a first name and a suburb.

02

Watch it assemble

The browser queries every source at once and streams matches into the sidebar, linking accounts, numbers and faces into a single subject.

03

Export the trace

Confirm the person, capture the evidence and export a cited, timestamped dossier — ready for the file, the client or the court.
